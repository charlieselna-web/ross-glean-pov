  • Moving from question to action across IT, supply chain, stores, and finance takes longer than it should — especially on exception handling and cross-functional decisions.
  • 2,700+ store managers and operations teams depend on tribal knowledge and manual escalation to find the right answer, the right person, or the right prior decision.

Where Glean Fits
  • One permission-aware AI layer across the tools Ross already operates — no rip-and-replace of Microsoft, Snowflake, or operational systems.
  • Search, Assistant, and Agents in a single governed platform — tuned to Ross's existing connectors and access controls.
  • Works alongside Microsoft Copilot as a complementary layer — deeper connectors into Jira, Confluence, Bitbucket, and operational systems M365 doesn't reach.
  • Deployable in days, not quarters — IT-owned, cross-functional, and designed to become a company-wide productivity initiative rather than a point solution.
Why This Matters for Ross
Why 01
Operational speed is a margin lever
Ross's off-price model runs on buying precision and execution speed. When teams can't move quickly from question to action — across planning, SOPs, or vendor context — that creates drag on the exact workflows that determine comp growth and cost efficiency.
Why 02
IT ownership scales this company-wide
A narrow engineering point solution doesn't move the needle. When IT owns the platform — covering service desk, project delivery, operations, and store systems — Glean becomes an enterprise initiative, not a departmental tool. That's the right play for an account at Ross's scale.
Why 03
New leadership = a window to set the standard
With a new CEO, new Board Chair, and a mandate around operational excellence and digital capability, this is exactly when platform decisions get made. The question isn't whether Ross will deploy an enterprise AI layer — it's whether Glean gets positioned early enough to shape that conversation.
